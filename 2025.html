Moodboard Description

That time of year thou mayst in me behold
When yellow leaves, or none, or few, do hang
Upon those boughs which shake against the cold,
Bare ruin'd choirs where late the sweet birds sang.
In me thou seest the twilight of such day
As after sunset fadeth in the west,
Which by and by black night doth take away.
Death's second self, that seals up all in rest.
In me thou seest the glowing of such fire
That on the ashes of his youth doth lie.
As the death-bed whereon it must expire,
Consum'd by that which it was nourished by.
This thou perceiv'st which makes thy love more strong,
to love that well which thou must leave ere long.
-# *By William Shakespeare*

Moodboard Description

The shifting colors of dawn breaking through delicate window panes should have been beautiful. And they would have been, if the silver chassis that they decorated was still affixed to its original body. They could still be, if viewed at a distance - far enough to overlook the pale scars that grafted metal to flesh, far enough to miss the icy chill that radiated off her tawny body. Far enough to be out of range of the silvered blades and the glowering ire of the sea bird’s unrelenting stare.
